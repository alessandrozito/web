Alessandro Zito

I am a Postdoctoral researcher in Biostatistics at Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health, under the supervision of Professor Jeff Miller. I recenty obtained my Ph.D. in Statistical Science at Duke University, in North Carolina. Prior to living in the United States in 2019, I studied Economics at Bocconi University, in Milan, Italy.

My current line of work is strongly motivated by application, which serves as a base for developing new statistical methodologies. At Duke, I worked under the supervision of Professor David Dunson within Project LIFEPLAN, an international research group whose aim is to quantify biodiversity across the globe.
